Demographics: The Driving Force of Almost Everything

Core Thesis Demographics
Key focal point

Demographics are the slow, predictable force that shapes long-run productivity, debt dynamics and growth, and therefore the background regime for markets, politics and society.

TL;DR

Age structures don’t grab headlines, but they decide most of the game. Economies with growing working-age populations can sustain higher growth and more debt. Ageing, shrinking societies face weaker productivity, heavier welfare burdens and a growing reliance on cheap money and financial engineering to keep the system moving.

Chain of events

Start with something simple: people drive demand and supply. The 25–50 cohort tends to be the most productive and the biggest consumer. When this group is expanding, you get strong labour supply, investment, household formation and risk-taking. When it plateaus or shrinks, you get the opposite.

As the share of retirees rises, more of national income is pulled toward pensions, healthcare and transfers. Governments either raise taxes, cut services, or lean on debt. Since 2000, most rich countries quietly chose option three: push up public debt, suppress interest rates and hope productivity or inflation will fix the real burden later.

Markets then “solve” the gap with asset inflation. With too much savings chasing too few real growth opportunities, capital piles into financial assets. Equities, property and bonds are bid up, not because future growth is amazing, but because the demographic tide forces capital to seek yield anywhere it can.

Summary & conclusion

Demographics don’t tell you what the S&P will do next quarter. They tell you what kind of world policymakers are trapped in over the next 10–30 years. Ageing societies with high debt and low productivity will lean on financial repression, negative real rates and repeated interventions.

That’s the iceberg beneath everything else on Devine Revalutions: liquidity, policy and politics are often just surface reactions to demographic realities that barely move, but never stop.
