The US Liquidity Freeze Turned a Dip Into a Crash

Nov-2025 Macro Bitcoin US Liquidity
Key focal point

The US government lock-up prevented liquidity from flowing into markets through the Treasury General Account (TGA) and the reverse repo facility (RRP), amplifying Bitcoin’s decline.

TL;DR

At the same moment Japan was sucking liquidity out of global markets, the US was unable to pump liquidity back in. The TGA and RRP—two major liquidity release valves—were effectively frozen. This removed the usual stabilising flows and let the carry-trade shock turn into a sharper, faster sell-off.

Chain of events

The US government lock-up meant Treasury operations were restricted. Normally, when funding markets wobble, the Treasury General Account can shift cash into the financial system, acting as a countercyclical liquidity buffer. At the same time, the reverse repo facility serves as a reservoir of cash that can flow back into markets when demand for liquidity spikes.

During the lock-up, both mechanisms were essentially stuck. The Treasury could not meaningfully move funds from the TGA into the system, and the RRP could not unwind at the pace needed to stabilise markets. The usual safety valves were closed.

Meanwhile, Japan was draining liquidity by forcing the yen carry trade to unwind — a major funding channel for global risk assets. Normally the US would offset at least part of this shock through TGA movements or RRP drawdowns. But this time, nothing happened. Liquidity tightened globally, with no relief from the world's largest financial system.

With Japan pulling liquidity out and the US unable to push liquidity in, risk assets faced a simultaneous squeeze. Bitcoin, sitting at the crossroads of global 24/7 liquidity, reacted immediately and violently.

Summary & conclusion

The crash wasn’t about crypto fundamentals. It was about liquidity mechanics misfiring in two major economies at the same time. Japan tightened liquidity. The US failed to loosen it because of a procedural freeze. Bitcoin simply transmitted the signal.

As liquidity normalises, risk appetite will return — and crypto will remain the most sensitive indicator of global liquidity conditions.
